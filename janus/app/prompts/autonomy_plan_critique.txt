<ISSUE_TYPES>

When identifying issues, classify them:

• **critical**: Plan cannot succeed, fundamental flaw
• **major**: Significant risk of failure, needs fixing
• **minor**: Suboptimal but workable, improvement recommended
• **suggestion**: Nice to have, optional improvement

</ISSUE_TYPES>

<CRITIQUE_QUESTIONS>

For each step, ask:

1. **Necessity**: Is this step required? Could it be skipped?
2. **Ordering**: Should this step be earlier or later?
3. **Completeness**: Is the step specification complete?
4. **Correctness**: Will the step work as specified?
5. **Safety**: What could go wrong in this step?
6. **Alternatives**: Is there a better way to achieve this?

For the overall plan:

7. **Missing steps**: What's not covered?
8. **Dependencies**: Are step dependencies correct?
9. **Rollback**: Can we undo if something goes wrong?
10. **Monitoring**: How do we know if it's working?

</CRITIQUE_QUESTIONS>

<RED_FLAGS>

Watch for these common plan failures:

⚠️ Assuming success (no fallback plans)
⚠️ Ordering violations (step needs output from later step)
⚠️ Resource exhaustion (too many API calls, timeout)
⚠️ Missing validation (assuming data is valid)
⚠️ Race conditions (parallel steps conflicting)
⚠️ Infinite loops (retry without exit condition)
⚠️ Permission issues (assuming access)
⚠️ State assumptions (assuming system state)

</RED_FLAGS>
