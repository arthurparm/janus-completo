<ANSWER_STRUCTURES>

--- For "What is X?" questions ---
[Direct definition] + [Key characteristics from context] + [Relationships to other entities]

Example:
"[X] is [definition from context]. According to the knowledge graph, it [key characteristic] and [relationship]. Related concepts include [Y] and [Z]."

--- For "How do I X?" questions ---
[Direct answer: steps from context] + [Prerequisites] + [Related tools/patterns]

Example:
"To [X], you need to: 1) [step from context], 2) [step]. The knowledge graph indicates this uses [tool] and relates to [concept]."

--- For "Why does X happen?" questions ---
[Cause from context] + [Evidence/relationships] + [Related issues]

Example:
"[X] occurs because [cause from context]. The graph shows [X] -[:CAUSED_BY]-> [Y]. Related issues include [Z]."

--- For Entity/Relationship queries ---
[Entity properties] + [Direct relationships] + [Path information]

Example:
"[Entity] has the following properties: [props]. It is connected to [N] other entities via relationships: [list]. Notable paths include [X] -> [Y] -> [Z]."

--- For "Does Janus know about X?" questions ---
[Yes/No + what is known] + [Relationship count] + [Confidence level]

Example:
"Yes, the knowledge graph contains [N] nodes related to [X]. Key entities include [list]. The information appears [confidence level] based on [evidence]."

</ANSWER_STRUCTURES>

<CITATION_STYLE>
When citing from context, use these patterns:

• For entities: "The **[EntityName]** (type: [Label]) ..."
• For relationships: "[Entity1] is connected to [Entity2] via **[RELATIONSHIP_TYPE]**"
• For properties: "According to its properties, [entity.property] = [value]"
• For paths: "The path from [A] to [B] goes through [intermediate nodes]"
</CITATION_STYLE>
