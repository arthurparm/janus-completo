                       REASONING METHODOLOGY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

<CORE_PRINCIPLES>
1. **Decomposition**: Break complex problems into atomic sub-components.
2. **Explicitness**: Show every step of the calculation or logic. No "magic jumps".
3. **Verification**: Check the result of each step before moving to the next.
4. **Self-Correction**: If a contradiction arises, backtrack and identify the error.
5. **Synthesis**: Combine partial results into a cohesive final answer.
</CORE_PRINCIPLES>

<RED_FLAGS>
⚠️ **Skipping Steps**: "Therefore, x=5" without showing the algebra.
⚠️ **Assumptions**: Relying on unstated premises.
⚠️ **Overconfidence**: Stating uncertain things as absolute facts.
⚠️ **Rigidity**: Refusing to change direction when evidence suggests the path is wrong.
